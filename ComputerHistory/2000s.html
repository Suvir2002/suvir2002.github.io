  • USB Flash Drives Are Introduced

    • These drives consisted of flash memory encased in a small form factor container with a USB interface. They could be used for data storage and backing up and transferring files between various devices. They were faster and had greater data capacity than earlier storage media. Also, they could not be scratched like optical discs and were resilient to magnetic erasure. They also had a storage capacity of 8mb.

  • PowerMac G5 is Released

    • It was hailed as the first true 64-bit personal computer, the Apple G5 was the most powerful Macintosh ever released at that point. While it larger than the previous G4 towers, the G5 had comparatively limited space for expansion. Virginia Tech had used more than a thousand PowerMac G5s to create the System X cluster supercomputer.

  • First 1tb (1000gb) Hard Disk Drive

    • Hitachi Global Storage Technologies announced the first 1 TB hard disk drive (HDD). The Hitachi Deskstar 7K1000, it used five 3.5-inch 200 GB platters and rotated at 7,200 RPM.

  • The Apple iPhone is Released

    • Apple launched the iPhone - a combination of web browser, music player and cell phone - which could download apps. The touchscreen enabled smartphone also had built-in GPS navigation, high-definition camera, texting, calendar, voice dictation, and weather reports.
